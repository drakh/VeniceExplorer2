Nobody's Land

Tomáš Džadoň
Arts

experiencing the in-between

Tomáš Džadoň uses the local refferences from his childhood in most of his visual art pieces. These are often the archetypes of the eastern block prefab buildings that meanwhile became a folklore. Not in all of his works he tackles the architectural topics too.

Yet when he extracts and materializes the pure nature of an in-between space of the usually narrow partition wall, with just a door opening, he talks directly to the architecture. By aggregating an archetypal door case into an extremely deep tunnel leading nowhere, he draws the attention directly to this Nobody's land.

 

Nobody's Land, a project by Tomáš DŽADOŇ (Poprad, SK / Prague, CZ)

 I extended the wall which divides the Jelení gallery in two small spaces so that it would fill almost the entire space. I enlarged the doorway on both sides so that visitors would have only the possibility to pass through. The “doorway” concession portal was originated. The gallery was filled by “passing.” Is it possible to somehow define change (transformation)? Whose space are we passing through?(Tomáš Džadoň, 2009)
